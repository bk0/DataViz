Project Dataset - Suicide Deaths

Dataset

The source of the dataset is the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention ("CDC"). I had to use the CDC website query tool to retrieve the data so there is no direct link. The link to the query landing page is located at http://wonder.cdc.gov/.

Per the data usage guidelines, I probably won't be able to visualize state-level data. The acceptable use of the data is as follows:
  • Use these data for health statistical reporting and analysis only.
  • For sub-national geography, not present or publish death counts of 9 or fewer or death rates based on counts of line or fewer (in figures, graphs, maps, tables, etc.).
  • Make no attempt to learn the identity of any person or establishment included in these data.
  • Make no disclosure or other use of the identity of any person or establishment discovered inadvertently and advise the NCHS Confidentiality Officer of any such discovery.


The dataset contains five numerical ("Year", "Age in Years", "Deaths", "Population", "Crude Rate") and two categorical fields ("Sex Race", "State"). If needed, I can re-query and add additional fields such as "State", "Region", "Ethnicity", and "Age Group". In its current structure, the dataset has approximately 60,000 rows. If this is too much data, I may use the suicide cause of death dataset which indicates the method of suicide (e.g., firearm, poisoning, falling, suffocation, cutting, etc.).

As for my motivation, I was originally intrigued by a table I saw after my Caltrain was severely delayed by a previous train hitting a car and killing the passenger. I read the news story the next day and it had a link about train suicides are tracked and indicated things like "head on track", "body on track", "standing on track", etc. I found the level of detail pretty amazing, but I couldn't find it again so, I went with suicides in general. The detail also has enough detail to give me flexibility in doing different visualizations.
